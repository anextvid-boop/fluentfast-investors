# Catalog of Questions to Answer

This document compiles the essential questions needed to finalize the investor documentation and fully build out the FluentFast Investor Portal. Answering these questions will provide the content needed to populate the landing page and pitch materials.

---

## 1. Product & Interaction Design
- **Q1.1 Physical Layout & UX**: How does the two-sided device interface work physically? Do players sit opposite each other sharing a single screen (with rotated UI elements like a board game), or do they connect two separate devices locally (e.g., via Bluetooth/local Wi-Fi)?
- **Q1.2 Screen Size / Device Type**: Is the app optimized for larger tablet screens (shared between players sitting side-by-side/opposite) or mobile phone screens?
- **Q1.3 Core Interaction Loop**: Describe a typical 60-second turn in a two-player session. What does Player A see and do, and how does Player B interact?
- **Q1.4 Offline / Solo Play**: Will there be a single-player mode to allow users to review vocabulary or practice when a partner is not physically present?

---

## 2. Marketing Angles & Variations
- **Q2.1 Product Verticals**: We have identified several key marketing angles:
  - **FluentFast Kids**: A version tailored for children (e.g., cartoon visual style, gamified vocabulary matching, interactive voice characters).
  - **FluentFast Travel**: Focused on rapid, phrasebook-style vocabulary for tourists (e.g., ordering food, checking in, directions).
  - **FluentFast Business**: Tailored for professional communication, networking, and industry-specific vocabulary.
- **Q2.2 Priorities**: Which of these variations (Kids, Travel, Business, or others) is the primary launch target, and which are scheduled for future expansion?
- **Q2.3 Language Selection**: Which languages will be supported at launch? (e.g., Spanish, French, English, Japanese?)

---

## 3. Educational Methodology
- **Q3.1 Immersion vs. Translation**: How does the co-located multiplayer format create an immersive environment? Does the app force users to speak only in the target language to solve puzzles?
- **Q3.2 Speech Recognition / AI Voice**: Do you plan to integrate AI voice recognition to automatically grade pronunciation, or will the grading rely strictly on peer-to-peer verification?
- **Q3.3 Curriculum Design**: How will the levels be designed? Are we using standard CEFR levels (A1, A2, B1, etc.), or a customized game-style progression?

---

## 4. Market & Competitors
- **Q4.1 Competitive Advantage**: Aside from local multiplayer, what prevents larger competitors (like Duolingo) from copying this feature? Is there a proprietary design or patentable hardware/interaction flow?
- **Q4.2 Viral Growth Mechanics**: How does the multiplayer element double as a marketing engine? (e.g., does the app encourage players to "invite a friend to play for free" as a guest, generating organic referrals?)

---

## 5. B2B & School Expansion ("Go Private")
- **Q5.1 Classroom Setup**: For schools, how does "Go Private" work? Does a teacher host a private local lobby, assigning students to pairs dynamically?
- **Q5.2 Teacher Controls**: What specific tools do teachers need in their dashboard? (e.g., student grading logs, custom curriculum builder, class performance analytics?)
- **Q5.3 Privacy Compliance**: Since schools deal with minors, what privacy controls (e.g., COPPA compliance, no tracking, local-only data) are required for the B2B school version?

---

## 6. Business & Monetization Model
- **Q6.1 Licensing**: How should pricing work? Will we offer a single subscription that includes a "guest pass" so friends can play for free, or will both players require an active account?
- **Q6.2 School Pricing**: How will the B2B school tier be priced? (e.g., per classroom license, per student subscription, or school-wide annual contract?)
- **Q6.3 Premium Upgrades**: What specific premium features will be paywalled? (e.g., advanced topics, voice recognition access, custom skins/avatars, ad-free experience?)
